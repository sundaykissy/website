About Me

I am a PhD Candidate with the Faculty of Computer Science, Dalhousie University. I obtained my BSc and MSc degrees from Usmanu Danfodiyo University and the University of Ilorin, Nigeria, respectively.

My research lies at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction, Virtual Reality, and Artificial Intelligence, with a particular focus on designing, developing, and evaluating immersive training systems. Specifically, my work investigates how VR can be used to improve skill acquisition in sports such as table tennis, where I have explored performance improvement, training strategies, coaching support, and methodological frameworks for evaluation. My studies contribute to understanding how AI-driven feedback, adaptive training protocols, and exergame design can enhance learning, motivation, and long-term engagement.

I have published peer-reviewed articles in leading international conferences and journals, and my research has been recognized within the VR and exergaming research communities. I have collaborated with interdisciplinary teams of researchers and industry partners, contributed as a reviewer for international venues, and co-organized academic activities.

Beyond academia, I have experience across multiple areas of Computer Science, including software engineering, web development, data science, and applied machine learning. I have also held teaching and mentorship roles, guiding students in advanced web development, VR technologies, and experimental research design.

Feel free to reach out to me to discuss potential collaborations in VR training, exergame evaluation, HCI, and AI-enhanced learning technologies :)
